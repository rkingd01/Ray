Albert Georgijev

My father's friends knew Dad as 'Fred', a shortened 'Alfred'. They wouldn't have known that he had been born 'Albert' in rural Ingria, West of Leningrad. Or that his parents were stripped of their farm, evicted and made homeless by a malevolent force. They would certainly not be aware of his family living 'rough' in woodland to avoid shelling until ejected by Russian troops. With the road to Leningrad blocked they returned to the wooded shelter only to be confronted by a German patrol whose leader provided assistance in escaping Stalin. Unfortunately, the journey to safety proved tragic.
