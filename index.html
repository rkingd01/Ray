Albert Georgijev

My father was known by most as 'Fred'. 'Alfred' according to his British passport. However, his birth name was 'Albert' and he was born and raised in Niitskovitsa village, Ingria, Russia. He was not proud of his Russian heritage and considered Estonia to be home.
A deep sadness in his past he kept to himself: a past where his family were stripped of all they owned and made homeless. There was no place for the family in Stalin's Russia.
A past where his mother did all that was possible to protect her children and in so doing jeopardised her own health.
