Albert Georgijev

My father's many friends called him 'Fred'. They may have guessed that this came from 'Alfred' but they had no idea that he had been born 'Albert' in rural Ingria, West of Leningrad. They wouldn't have known that he'd seen his parents humiliated and stripped of their farm. They wouldn't have known that his family were evicted and made homeless so that Collective fields could be enlarged.

Amidst a period of fighting his family lived 'rough' in a forest until they were ejected by a Russian patrol. After aborting their travel to Leningrad they defiantly returned to the forest only to be discovered by a German patrol which in contrast provided assistance.

For two days the family made their way out of Stalin's Russia to safety in Estonia. At Narva (Ee) he witnessed his mother collapsing with the strain of getting her seven children out of Stalin's reach, too weak to recover. Vengeful, as soon as he was able Albert, Russian by birth, sought to join the Estonian division of the Waffen SS.
