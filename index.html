Albert Georgijev

My father's co-workers and friends called my dad Fred. They didn't know that his real name was Alfred and that he had been born Albert.

Neither did they know that as a boy...

...he'd lived in rural Ingria, West of Leningrad and seen his parents stripped of their farm;

...his family were made homeless to make a Collective field larger;

...he and his family lived 'rough' in a forest until they were threatened by a Russian patrol;

...he and the family were discovered hiding in another forest by a German patrol which provided assistance;

...he and his family walked for two days to get out of Stalin's Russia to safety in Estonia.

...he witnessed his mother collapsing and unable to recover from the strain of getting her children out of Stalin's reach. As soon as he was able he joined the German army that had rid Estonia of their Russian occupiers.
